# HR Dashboard – Tableau Project
 
## Project Overview
This project is an interactive **Human Resources (HR) Dashboard** built using **Tableau**. It analyzes employee data to help HR managers and business leaders make better, data-driven decisions about their workforce.
 
The dashboard was built as a practice project based on a real-world HR business case, using a simulated HR dataset that includes employee demographics, job details, salary information, and attrition (employee turnover) data.
 
## Business Problem
As an HR manager, understanding your workforce is critical for good decision-making. This dashboard answers key business questions such as:
- How many employees were hired, are currently active, or have left the company?
- What are the hiring and termination trends over time?
- How is the workforce distributed across departments, job titles, and locations?
- What does the age, gender, and education breakdown of employees look like?
- How do salaries compare across departments, genders, and education levels?
## Key Features
- **Overview Dashboard** – total headcount, hires vs terminations over time, department and job title breakdown, HQ vs branch comparison
- **Demographics Dashboard** – gender ratio, age distribution, education level, employee location by city/state
- **Income Analysis Dashboard** – salary comparison across departments, gender, and education level
- **Interactive Filters** – drill down by department, job title, gender, and location
- **Detailed Employee Records** – individual-level view of employee data for deeper analysis
## Tools & Technologies Used
- **Tableau** (Desktop / Public) – for data visualization and dashboard design
- **CSV Data Source** – HR employee dataset (demographics, job info, salary, attrition)
- **Data Cleaning & Modeling** – performed within Tableau to prepare the raw data for analysis
## Dataset
The dataset is representing typical HR data found in real companies, including:
- Employee ID, name, gender, age
- Department and job title
- Hire date and termination date (if applicable)
- Location (city, state)
- Education level
- Salary
- Performance rating

## Key Insights
- Employee headcount and attrition vary noticeably across departments, with some departments showing a higher share of terminations than others — useful for identifying where retention efforts should be focused.
- The headquarters (HQ) location holds a significantly larger share of employees compared to branch locations, reflecting a centralized workforce structure.
- Salary levels show a clear relationship with education level, with employees holding higher degrees generally earning more on average.
- The gender distribution across the company is not perfectly balanced, which is visible clearly through the demographics view.
- Hiring and termination trends over the years reveal specific periods of workforce growth and contraction, which can help HR plan future hiring more effectively.
